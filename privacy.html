BBQ Captain Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 1, 2026

BBQ Captain (“the app,” “we,” “us”) is built privacy-first. This policy explains how the app handles your data. The short version: your cooking data stays on your device, and we don’t run a server that collects it.

Data stored on your device

Everything you create in the app — your cooks, temperature readings, notes, photos, custom profiles, and settings — is stored locally on your device using Apple’s on-device storage. We do not operate a backend that receives this data, and it is never transmitted to us.

Purchases

BBQ Captain offers a one-time “Pro” in-app purchase. Purchases are processed by Apple. To deliver and restore your Pro entitlement, we use RevenueCat, Inc. as our purchase infrastructure. RevenueCat receives purchase and receipt information associated with an anonymous, randomly generated identifier. This information is used solely to validate and restore your purchase. It is not linked to your name or identity, and it is not used for advertising or cross-app tracking.

  • Apple’s privacy policy: apple.com/legal/privacy
  • RevenueCat’s privacy policy: revenuecat.com/privacy

What we do not do

  • We do not collect personal information.
  • We do not use analytics or advertising SDKs.
  • We do not track you across other apps or websites.
  • We do not sell or share your data.

Notifications

The app may schedule local notifications on your device — for example, when to put your meat on, phase and spritz reminders, or a rest timer. These are generated locally on your device and are not routed through any server of ours.

Photos

If you attach a photo to a cook (a Pro feature), it is copied into the app’s private storage on your device. Photos are not uploaded anywhere.

Children’s privacy

The app is rated 4+ and does not knowingly collect personal data from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The “Last updated” date above will reflect any changes.
